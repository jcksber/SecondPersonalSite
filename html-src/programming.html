Code Combat

This is a really fun and interactive way to learn the basic concepts of programming. It's a web application that keeps you interested with new enemies, new challenges, and customizable characters. Most importantly, it's probably the best application for the initial understanding of what exactly programming is and how it can be useful and exciting.

CodeHS

Developed by a group of Stanford graduates, this website is the future for people who want to learn how to code before college. On their site, they use a coding language written specifically for students to make the initial "hurdle" of getting into the game much easier. Tons of online tutors will help you any time of the day with both small and large questions; their staff loves to code! They focus on teaching high school classes, but offer individual purchases for the inspired.

Codecademy

I used Codecademy to learn the basics of HTML and their UI (User Interface) is absolutely phenomenal. It's incredibly easy to use and they keep you active with slightly more challenging problems each time. They offer plenty of other languages including Python and JavaScript.

Why Create A Website?

     I personally believe that the evolution of our society is making designing your own website (or atleast having one) a necessity for every job seeker. It is one of the easiest ways to make your resume available and your contact information accessible. A piece of paper with your interests on it doesn't say much about yourself, but a personal website can have the content you want, and its design speaks directly to your personality.

     To get started designing and creating your personal website, check out this step by step process that got me to where I was about 2 months ago. After, spend some time looking through this website which was created by one of the most influential web designers of our time, Chris Coyier. Both of these tools should be enough to get you up and running, but in the end it's all about the time you put in and your passion for it. Good luck, and happy coding!
